# Vision — Lattice

## Purpose

Lattice exists to give cross-functional product teams a single place where work, decisions, and context live together — eliminating the fragmented conversations, lost context, and duplicated effort that slow every team down. Lattice is the shared brain that turns scattered teamwork into coordinated progress.

## Vision

Lattice will become the default workspace where product teams think, decide, and build together. Every conversation is connected to the work it drives. Every decision is traceable to the context that informed it. Every team member, regardless of role or location, can see the full picture without chasing people down.

## Problem

Cross-functional product teams — design, engineering, product, and operations — lose hours every week to context switching between tools, searching for decisions buried in chat threads, and re-explaining context that was never captured in the first place. A mid-size product team reports spending 8 hours per week per person on coordination overhead: finding the latest design spec, understanding why a decision was made, or aligning on what changed since the last sync. The cost compounds — misalignment leads to rework, rework leads to missed deadlines, and missed deadlines erode trust across the team.

## Solution

Lattice brings work, decisions, and conversations into a single connected space. Teams create structured work items linked to the discussions that shaped them, capture decisions with the context that informed them, and maintain a living record that stays current without manual effort. The product adapts to how each team already works rather than forcing a new process.

## Target Audience

Lattice serves cross-functional product teams who need to coordinate complex work across roles and disciplines. These teams prioritize clarity and shared context over individual productivity tools, and their team leads and operations managers make adoption decisions based on coordination efficiency and reduced rework.

## Platform Pillars

### Connected Work

Every piece of work is linked to the conversations and decisions that shaped it, so context is never lost.

### Living Context

The record stays current as work evolves — no manual status updates or duplicate entries required.

### Team Clarity

Every team member can see the full picture of what is happening, why, and what comes next.

## Philosophy

### Clarity Over Cleverness

Every feature should be immediately understandable to the team using it, even if that means a simpler implementation.

### Context Is the Product

The value of Lattice is not the tools it provides but the context it preserves — every decision should be traceable to the information that informed it.

### Work Together, Stay Independent

Teams should be able to collaborate seamlessly without being forced into a single rigid workflow.

## Guiding Principles

These principles guide every downstream decision about Lattice's design, architecture, and features.

### Link Before You Describe

When adding a new piece of work, connect it to existing context first — related discussions, prior decisions, or parent goals — before writing a description. Context accumulation beats context creation.

### Make the Implicit Explicit

If a team is likely to wonder "why did we decide this?" or "what changed?", capture that proactively rather than waiting for someone to ask.

### Preserve Flow, Don't Force Structure

Structuring work should happen within the flow of collaboration, not as a separate step that breaks momentum.

## Success Criteria

* Teams report spending less than 3 hours per week on coordination overhead — down from 8.
* At least 80% of product decisions are captured with their context within 24 hours of being made.
* New team members can understand a project's history and current state without asking a single person.
